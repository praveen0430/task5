History

Cricket's origins are debated, but it's believed to have emerged in southern England during the 16th century,
evolving from a game played by children in the Weald region.
The first recorded reference to cricket is in 1597, and by the 18th century,
it had become a popular sport, with the "Laws of Cricket" codified in 1744.

Rules

Cricket is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of 11 players, aiming to score more runs than the opposing team by batting and fielding, with various ways to get batsmen out.
. Game Setup & Objective:
Teams: Two teams of 11 players each.
Objective: Score more runs than the opposing team.
Innings: Each team bats and bowls in turns, with a set number of overs (deliveries bowled by a bowler).
Toss: A coin toss decides which team bats first.
